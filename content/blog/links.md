## üåç Global Frameworks for Ethical and Trustworthy AI

Artificial Intelligence has inspired multiple **international frameworks** that aim to balance **innovation** with **ethical responsibility**.  
While some are **binding laws**, most function as **soft-law guidelines** shaping policy, regulation, and organizational practice.

### üèõÔ∏è OECD Council Recommendation on Artificial Intelligence (OECD/LEGAL/0449)

The **OECD Council Recommendation on Artificial Intelligence**, adopted in **2019** and revised in **2023‚Äì2024**, is the **first intergovernmental standard on AI**.  
It establishes the foundation for **trustworthy, human-centric AI** across 46+ countries, including **EU and UK** adherents.

**Type:** OECD Recommendation (soft law, *in force*)  
**Adopted:** 22 May 2019 ¬∑ **Revised:** 8 Nov 2023 and 3 May 2024  
**Adherents:** All OECD Members + several Non-Members (e.g., Brazil, Singapore, Romania, Argentina, Saudi Arabia)  
**Purpose:** Promote a **human-centric, trustworthy AI** that respects the rule of law, human rights, and democratic values‚Äîwhile enabling innovation and international interoperability.

---

#### üîπ Core Principles (for all stakeholders)

1. **Inclusive Growth, Sustainable Development & Well-Being**  
   AI should promote inclusive growth and environmental sustainability.

2. **Respect for Rule of Law, Human Rights & Democratic Values**  
   AI actors must uphold fairness, equality, privacy, human dignity, and labour rights.

3. **Transparency & Explainability**  
   Ensure stakeholders understand when and how AI affects them, and allow for challenge or review of AI-driven outcomes.

4. **Robustness, Security & Safety**  
   AI systems must function safely and be overrideable, repairable, or decommissioned if risks occur.

5. **Accountability**  
   AI actors are responsible for outcomes, ensuring traceability and systematic risk management across the AI lifecycle.

---

#### üîπ Governmental Policy Recommendations (for Adherents)

1. **Invest in AI Research & Development** ‚Äî Encourage public and private investment, open science, and bias-free open data.  
2. **Foster Inclusive AI Ecosystems** ‚Äî Support data-sharing, infrastructure, and knowledge exchange.  
3. **Shape Interoperable Governance** ‚Äî Develop agile, outcome-based, cross-border policy environments.  
4. **Build Human Capacity & Labour Readiness** ‚Äî Equip people with skills, ensure fair transitions, and promote safe, high-quality jobs.  
5. **Strengthen International Cooperation** ‚Äî Collaborate on standards, measurement indicators, and responsible AI governance.

---

#### üîπ 2023‚Äì2024 Revisions Highlights

- Updated **AI System definition** to explicitly include **Generative AI**  
- Expanded focus on **misinformation, disinformation, and information integrity**  
- Added explicit emphasis on **environmental sustainability**  
- Required mechanisms for **override, repair, and safe decommissioning**  
- Reinforced **responsible business conduct** and **traceability**  
- Promoted **cross-jurisdictional interoperability** in AI governance

---

#### üîπ Global Impact and Alignment

- Basis for the **G20 AI Principles (2019)**  
- Aligns with **EU Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI**  
- Complements **EU AI Act** (binding) and **NIST AI RMF** (technical implementation)  
- Forms part of the **UK‚Äôs AI regulation framework** through OECD adherence  
- Supported by **OECD.AI Policy Observatory** and the **ONE AI Expert Network**

### üß≠ Key Takeaways

- **Binding Law:**  
  - The **European AI Act** is currently the only **legally enforceable regulation**.

- **Soft-Law Guidelines:**  
  - **OECD**, **Asilomar**, **EU Ethics**, **NIST**, and **WEF** frameworks shape **policy and best practice** worldwide.

- **Geographical Relevance:**  
  - **EU:** AI Act (binding), AI HLEG & OECD (guiding)  
  - **UK:** OECD & NIST (guiding)  
  - **Global:** WEF & Asilomar (voluntary, shared ethical basis)
