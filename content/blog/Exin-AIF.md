## **Domain 1 – Introduction to AI and Historical Development (15%)**

### **Task 1.1 – Identify key definitions of AI terms**

#### Objectives

* Define human intelligence, artificial intelligence, and machine learning.
* Explain the concept of the scientific method as applied to AI.
* Recognize how these foundational definitions relate to the development of AI systems.

---

### **Task 1.2 – Describe key milestones in AI development**

#### Objectives

* Identify and describe major events such as the Asilomar Principles, the Dartmouth Conference (1956), and AI winters.
* Explain how big data, IoT, and large language models (LLMs) influenced modern AI.
* Understand how these milestones shaped today’s AI ecosystem and public awareness.

---

### **Task 1.3 – Describe different types of AI**

#### Objectives

* Distinguish between **narrow (weak)** and **general (strong)** AI.
* Provide real-world examples of narrow AI applications (e.g., image recognition, speech recognition, translation, virtual assistants).
* Explain the theoretical goal and characteristics of general AI.

---

### **Task 1.4 – Explain the impact of AI on society**

#### Objectives

* Identify ethical, social, economic, and environmental impacts of AI.
* Describe frameworks such as Floridi & Cowls’ principles and the UK AI principles.
* Explain implications for sustainability, energy use, employment, and reskilling.
* Relate AI’s evolution to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and the EU AI Act (2024).

---

### **Task 1.5 – Describe sustainability measures to reduce AI’s environmental impact**

#### Objectives

* Explain Green IT initiatives and data-center efficiency measures.
* Describe sustainable supply chain and algorithm choices.
* Identify how low-code/no-code tools and environmental monitoring reduce AI’s carbon footprint.

---

## **Domain 2 – Ethical and Legal Considerations (15%)**

### **Task 2.1 – Describe ethical concerns in AI including bias and privacy**

#### Objectives

* Define ethics and distinguish ethics from law.
* Recognize key ethical concerns: bias, discrimination, privacy, employment impact, autonomous weapons and vehicles.
* Explain how ethical reasoning influences trust and public acceptance of AI.

---

### **Task 2.2 – Describe the importance of guiding principles in ethical AI development**

#### Objectives

* Explain principles of safety, transparency, fairness, accountability, and contestability.
* Identify legislation and frameworks such as the UK AI principles and AI governance structures.
* Describe how AI steering committees and organizational policies ensure responsible AI deployment.

---

### **Task 2.3 – Explain strategies for addressing ethical challenges in AI projects**

#### Objectives

* Identify ethical challenges (self-interest, conflicts of interest, intimidation, advocacy).
* Apply strategies for mitigation: transparency, openness, trustworthiness, explainability.
* Relate ethical risk frameworks to AI project lifecycle management.

---

### **Task 2.4 – Explain the role of regulation in AI**

#### Objectives

* Explain why AI regulation is needed to ensure accountability and safety.
* Identify key laws and standards (Data Protection Act 2018, UK GDPR, ISO, NIST, WCAG).
* Describe risks of unregulated AI and benefits of a governed ecosystem.

---

### **Task 2.5 – Explain the process of risk management in AI**

#### Objectives

* Define risk and risk management in the context of AI.
* Apply methods such as SWOT, PESTLE, and Cynefin to AI projects.
* Explain risk mitigation strategies (ownership, accountability, stakeholder involvement).
* Relate AI risk management to regulations and ethical standards.

---

## **Domain 3 – Enablers of AI (15%)**

### **Task 3.1 – List common examples of AI**

#### Objectives

* Identify examples of AI applications (human-compatible, wearable, edge, IoT, personal care, autonomous vehicles, generative tools).
* Recognize AI integration in everyday devices and services.

---

### **Task 3.2 – Describe the role of robotics in AI**

#### Objectives

* Define robot and differentiate between intelligent and non-intelligent robots.
* Identify types of robots (industrial, personal, autonomous, nanobot, humanoid).
* Explain robotic process automation (RPA) and its business impact.

---

### **Task 3.3 – Describe machine learning**

#### Objectives

* Define machine learning and its relation to AI and data science.
* Explain neural networks, deep learning, and large language models.
* Identify how ML enables systems to improve through experience.

---

### **Task 3.4 – Identify common machine learning concepts**

#### Objectives

* Describe key ML concepts: prediction, object recognition, classification, clustering, and recommendation systems.
* Recognize real-world applications of these techniques.

---

### **Task 3.5 – Describe supervised and unsupervised learning**

#### Objectives

* Explain differences between supervised, unsupervised, and semi-supervised learning.
* Identify examples and use cases for each approach.
* Describe how data labeling affects algorithm training.

---

## **Domain 4 – Finding and Using Data in AI (20%)**

### **Task 4.1 – Describe key data terms**

#### Objectives

* Define big data, data visualization, structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data.
* Explain how data types support different AI applications.

---

### **Task 4.2 – Describe the characteristics of data quality and its importance in AI**

#### Objectives

* Identify the five data quality dimensions: accuracy, completeness, uniqueness, consistency, timeliness.
* Explain how poor data quality leads to bias and model errors.
* Relate data quality to trust and decision-making.

---

### **Task 4.3 – Explain risks in handling AI data and how to minimize them**

#### Objectives

* Recognize bias, misinformation, processing restrictions, and legal limitations.
* Describe risk mitigation measures (diverse data sources, fairness metrics, expert review, GDPR compliance).
* Relate the scientific method to learning from data errors and iteration.

---

### **Task 4.4 – Describe the purpose and use of big data**

#### Objectives

* Explain how big data supports insight, process optimization, and personalization.
* Describe its role in decision-making and strategic improvement.

---

### **Task 4.5 – Explain data visualization techniques and tools**

#### Objectives

* Identify different visualization methods (written, verbal, pictorial, sound-based, dashboards, infographics, VR/AR).
* Explain how effective visualization supports communication and analysis.

---

### **Task 4.6 – Describe key generative AI terms**

#### Objectives

* Define generative AI and large language models (LLMs).
* Recognize how these models generate new content from training data.

---

### **Task 4.7 – Describe the purpose and use of generative AI including LLMs**

#### Objectives

* Explain how LLMs are trained on large datasets to predict and generate text.
* Define prompt engineering and natural language processing (NLP).
* Identify common use cases (text generation, translation, image creation).

---

### **Task 4.8 – Describe how data is used to train AI in the machine learning process**

#### Objectives

* Outline the machine learning stages: problem definition, data selection, pre-processing, visualization, model selection, training, testing, and iteration.
* Explain how feedback and experience improve model accuracy.

---

## **Domain 5 – Using AI in Your Organization (20%)**

### **Task 5.1 – Identify opportunities for AI in an organization**

#### Objectives

* Recognize use cases for automation and efficiency gains.
* Identify opportunities in content creation and repetitive processes.

---

### **Task 5.2 – List the contents and structure of a business case**

#### Objectives

* Identify sections of a business case: introduction, summary, current state, options, analysis, recommendations, appendices.
* Explain how a business case supports decision-making and funding approval.

---

### **Task 5.3 – Identify and categorize stakeholders in an AI project**

#### Objectives

* Define stakeholders and their influence.
* Use tools like the Power/Interest grid and Stakeholder Wheel for classification.
* Explain the importance of managing stakeholder engagement.

---

### **Task 5.4 – Describe project management approaches**

#### Objectives

* Compare Agile, Waterfall, and Hybrid methods.
* Identify how each approach suits different AI project types.

---

### **Task 5.5 – Identify risks, costs, and benefits of a proposed AI solution**

#### Objectives

* Perform basic risk and cost-benefit analyses.
* Define risk appetite and apply mitigation strategies (accept, avoid, transfer, mitigate).
* Recognize financial and socio-economic impacts including the triple bottom line.

---

### **Task 5.6 – Describe ongoing governance activities when implementing AI**

#### Objectives

* Explain AI governance across compliance, risk management, and lifecycle governance.
* Identify monitoring and control mechanisms for responsible AI use.

---

## **Domain 6 – Future Planning and Impact – Human Plus Machine (15%)**

### **Task 6.1 – Describe roles and career opportunities presented by AI**

#### Objectives

* Identify AI-specific roles (e.g., ML engineer, data scientist, AI ethics specialist).
* Recognize how existing roles evolve through AI adoption and training.
* Understand AI’s impact on skills development and job design.

---

### **Task 6.2 – Identify AI uses in the real world**

#### Objectives

* Describe AI applications across industries (marketing, healthcare, finance, transport, education, manufacturing, entertainment, IT).
* Explain how AI enhances processes, products, and user experiences.

---

### **Task 6.3 – Explain AI’s impact on society and its future**

#### Objectives

* Discuss benefits and challenges of AI at societal level.
* Evaluate economic and environmental impacts (job losses, sustainability, climate change).
* Describe potential future developments in AI (advanced algorithms, data availability, computing power).

---

### **Task 6.4 – Describe consciousness and its impact on ethical AI**

#### Objectives

* Define human and AI consciousness and related theories (Seth, Kurzweil Singularity).
* Distinguish between functional capability and true sentience.
* Discuss ethical implications of artificial consciousness and human-AI interaction authenticity.
